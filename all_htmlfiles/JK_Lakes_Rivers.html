Dal Lake

Dal Lake is one of the most picturesque lakes in Jammu and Kashmir and is known as Srinagar's Jewel or the Jewel in Kashmir's Crown. This lake, nestled among the majestic Himalayas, is famous for its amazing houseboats and refreshing shikara rides. Seeing the sunset while sailing over these wooden boats is an unforgettable experience. Dal Lake is the second-largest lake in Kashmir Valley and is also mythologically significant. Another attraction of this lake is the sacred Shiva Temple. Swimming, kayaking, surfing, canoeing, and fishing are among the watersports available here.
Location: Boulevard Road, Srinagar 190001, Jammu and Kashmir

Alpather Lake

Alpather Lake is the most prominent attraction of Gulmarg. Situated at an altitude of 4380m, this lake sits charmingly at the foot of Apharwat Peak. It is a small alpine lake that required around 2 hours of trek from the Gondola phase second.
Location: Apharwat peak,Gulmarg, Jammu and Kashmir

Pangong Lake

The lake that was made famous by the Bollywood movie “Three Idiots”, Pangong Lake is one of the most beautiful lakes in the whole of Kashmir. An extremely picturesque spot for bird watching, this lake is enchanting in its personality. One can also camp near it and enjoy the various migratory birds that flock here.
Location: Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir, India; Rutog County, Tibet, China

Tulian Lake

Located 3353 m above sea level, Tulian Lake is a picturesque and panoramic place among the numerous lakes in Jammu and Kashmir. This lake in Pahalgam is nestled between the Zanskar and Pir Panjal mountain ranges and provides breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks along with crystal clear waters. With floating ice chunks and meadows filled with pine forests, this lake offers a surreal experience. The hike to the lake is strenuous, with several steep ascents, but the breathtaking mountain scenery and tranquil surroundings make every effort worthwhile.
Location: Pahalgam, Kashmir Valley, Jammu and Kashmir

Mansabal Lake

Renowned as the 'Supreme Gem of all Lakes in Kashmir', it is one of the most gorgeous lakes in Jammu and Kashmir. This is Kashmir's deepest lake, and the presence of lovely lotus blossoms adds to its allure. It got its name after the holy lake of Mansarovar and the 'Jharokha' erected by Nur Jahan in the Mughal Garden overlooks the lake. Darogabagh Fort and the Jharokha Garden, which are located on the northern side of Lake Baikal are its other attractions. Tourists can also go boating, water skiing, fishing, and more while enjoying the area's natural splendor.
Location: Safapora, Ganderbal, Jammu and Kashmir

Nigeen Lake

Nigeen Lake is an offshoot of Dal Lake connected by a small causeway. The lake is located east of Srinagar in the foothills of the Zabarwan Mountain. It is bounded on the south by Shankaracharya Hill and on the west by Hari Parbat. Nigeen Lake is surrounded by lovely willow and poplar trees, it is amongst the most popular lakes in Jammu and Kashmir that draw tourists for its serenity and surroundings. Home to a large number of houseboats and shikaras, it offers thrilling opportunities for water skiing, motorboating, sailing, and much more.
Location: Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir.

Wular Lake

Wular Lake, one of Asia's largest freshwater lakes, is a must-see attraction in Kashmir near Srinagar. Boating and various water sports events bring adventurers here to enjoy thrills in beautiful surroundings. Wular Lake, one of India's 26 wetlands fed by the Jhelum River, attracts both locals and tourists. This attraction is ideal for those seeking a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of city life. A walk around the lake is relaxing and rejuvenating, but you can also explore the area by boat and pedal your way through the waters.
Location: Bandipora District, Srinagar, Jammu and Kashmir

Gangabal Lake

Situated at 3570 m at the base of Mount Harmukh, Gangabal Lake is home to a variety of fishes. Lush meadows and towering mountains wrapped in whimsical clouds surround the lake. Apart from fishing, one can enjoy nature and horse riding here at Harmukh Ganga Lake. From Zaj Pass, you'll be able to see the lake in all its glory. It is one of the most unique lakes in Jammu and Kashmir in terms of views for nature lovers and activities for adventure seekers.
Location: Ganderbal, Jammu and Kashmir

Nund Kol Lake

Nundkol is one of the renowned Gangabal Lake's twin lakes. It is located at the base of Mt. Harmukh, which is revered by the people as a sacred mountain. It is an oligotrophic mountain lake that offers breathtaking views and is an ideal camping spot. During the summer, many villagers travel from Naranag to this lake and spend a few days camping on the bank of this lovely lake. It is also famous amongst trekkers and anglers for its beautiful views and abundance of fish.
Location: Ganderbal, Jammu and Kashmir

Gadsar Lake

Gadsar Lake is one of the most scenic alpine Jammu Kashmir lakes. Situated at an elevation of 13,750 ft, this lake is ideal for nature photographers to capture some extraordinary shots. The lakes' emerald water reflects the surrounding mountains, clouds, and trees, presenting a sight to behold. The lake is full of brown trout, offering a great opportunity for fishermen to get out on the water and have some fun. It lies on the Great Lakes Trek and is an incredible place to experience some real thrills.
Location: Sonmarg, Ganderbal, Kashmir Valley, Jammu and Kashmir

Vishansar Lake

One of the high-altitude lakes in Jammu and Kashmir, Vishansar Lake is located near Sonmarg at a height of 3,710m. While one side of the lake is dominated by imposing peaks, the other is bordered by lush green meadows. Besides its natural beauty, the summer fishing prospects for trout and mahseer attract tourists. Winter freezes over the lake, making it absolutely stunning. If you're looking for an opportunity to recharge your spirit and relax your thoughts, here is the place for you.
Location: Near Nichnai Pass, Sonamarg, Jammu and Kashmir
